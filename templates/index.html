Index Descriptions

SPY

The S&P 500 Index, or the Standard & Poor's 500, is a market-capitalization-weighted index of the 500 largest U.S. publicly traded companies. It is a float-weighted index, meaning company market capitalizations are adjusted by the number of shares available for public trading.

DIA

The Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA) is a widely-watched benchmark index in the U.S. for blue-chip stocks. The DJIA is a price-weighted index that tracks 30 large, publicly-owned companies trading on the New York Stock Exchange and the NASDAQ.

QQQ

Nasdaq is a global electronic marketplace for buying and trading securities. It was the world's first electronic exchange. It operates in 25 markets, one clearinghouse, and five central securities depositories in the United States and Europe. The Nasdaq also lists popular cryptocurrencies.
